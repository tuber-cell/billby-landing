How to Calculate GST — Simple Steps

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a comprehensive indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services in India. For freelancers and small businesses, understanding how to calculate GST correctly is essential for compliance and accurate invoicing.

To calculate GST on an invoice: Multiply the taxable value by the applicable GST rate. For intra-state sales, split the total tax into two equal parts — CGST (Central GST) and SGST (State GST). For inter-state sales, charge the full rate as IGST (Integrated GST).

Example (Intra-state, 18% GST):

  • Taxable value: ₹10,000
  • CGST: ₹900 (9%)
  • SGST: ₹900 (9%)
  • Total invoice amount: ₹11,800

Why use a GST calculator? Manual calculations are prone to errors, especially when dealing with multiple rates or reverse GST calculations. Our free GST calculator handles CGST, SGST, and IGST splits instantly — so you can focus on growing your business.

What is reverse GST? Reverse GST (or reverse charge) calculates the original taxable amount from a total that already includes GST. This is useful when you know the final price but need to figure out the base value and tax components.
